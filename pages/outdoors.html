When I'm not running, I love getting outdoors and cycling, hiking, backpacking, and taking photographs, to name a few!

Some Things I've Done

When I was sent home from college due to COVID-19 pandemic in the spring of 2020, I took the long way home from Southern California to Washington, visiting the Mighty 5 National Parks in Utah along with 3 National Monuments and 2 State Parks on a 13 day road trip, hiking, camping, climbing, running, and photographing along the way.

In August 2019, I tackled my first bike touring trip, cycling 400 miles over 5 days from Tokyo to Joetsu to Niigata to Sado Island. Though I was ill prepared, having barely biked over the past few years, using a borrowed bike, and not having a route planned, I loved every minute of it, from the beautiful rivers and coastlines, the long climbs and epic descents through the mountains, and the super friendly and helpful people!

Having climbed Mount Fuji many times that summer, in August 2019 several friends and I decided to run, walk, and hike Mount Fuji from sea to summit, starting at the coast and climbing to the highest point in Japan in one go, covering more than a marathon in distance and climbing more than 12,000ft! I would love to go back and try to set a Fastest Known Time on the route in the future!

During the summer of 2019, I worked as a bilingual guide for Fuji Mountain Guides leading international guests on one- and two-day trips to the summit of Mount Fuji. Getting to work with amazing guides, meet so many interesting guests, and climb this iconic mountain many times made for one of the most fun and rewarding summers of my life!

In August 2018, I backpacked the Loowit Trail that circumnavigates Mount Saint Helens with a high school friend. The geography and flora of the mountain is extremely diverse, with lush forests and rivers, large volcanic rock fields, and the barren moonscape caused by the recent eruption in 1980. I would love to go back someday, and maybe even run it in a day!
